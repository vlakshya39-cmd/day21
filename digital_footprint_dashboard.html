WOW insights
Google knows more about you than your family (fact basis)
With Search + Pay + Photos + YouTube + Google.com, Google can cross-reference what you search, what you buy, who you photograph, and what you watch — building a more accurate profile than most humans could.
TikTok collects face and voice data by default (est.)
TikTok's privacy policy has stated collection of biometric identifiers in some regions. Combined with its ByteDance parent company's jurisdiction, this is considered higher-risk than comparable Western platforms.
Your payment data is your most sensitive asset
Google Pay transaction metadata (not amounts alone, but merchant, frequency, and timing) enables highly accurate life-event detection — salary day, medical visits, travel, and relationship changes.
WhatsApp metadata reveals more than content
Even with end-to-end encryption, WhatsApp's parent Meta collects who you message, how often, at what times, and on what device. This social graph alone is highly valuable to advertisers.
Privacy improvement plan
1
Review Google account data controls
Go to myaccount.google.com → Data & Privacy → turn off Web & App Activity, Location History, and YouTube History where possible. Delete past data periodically.
2
Audit TikTok permissions
Revoke microphone, camera, and contact access when not actively using the app. Consider using TikTok in a browser instead of the native app to limit device-level access.
3
Enable Instagram and Snapchat privacy modes
Set accounts to private, limit data sharing with third-party advertisers in both apps' settings, and disable location tagging on posts.
4
Use Signal for sensitive conversations
WhatsApp's encryption is strong, but metadata is still collected by Meta. Signal (open source, non-commercial) collects minimal metadata for truly private communication.
5
Reduce Google ecosystem concentration
Consider alternatives: DuckDuckGo for search, ProtonMail for email, and an offline gallery app for photos. Even replacing one Google service reduces cross-service profiling significantly.
6
Check app permissions quarterly
Review which apps have access to camera, microphone, contacts, and location on your phone. Revoke any permission that isn't required for core functionality.
Final verdict
Significant exposure, weak protection
Your digital footprint spans 15 services across 6 corporations. Google alone touches your searches, payments, photos, and video consumption — making it a single point of failure for your privacy. The combination of financial data (Google Pay), biometrics (Photos, Snapchat, TikTok), and behavioural data (YouTube, Search) creates a highly accurate digital twin that advertisers and data brokers can use for profiling.
